Before you report — gather this info

Keep these details ready to ensure the fastest response from investigators.

Scammer Identity
Collect any phone numbers, UPI IDs, or Bank account details used by the criminal.
Hard Evidence
Save screenshots of WhatsApp/Facebook chats, SMS threads, or call recordings.
Transaction Data
Keep your Transaction ID (UTR), exact amount lost, and the timestamp of the event.
Your Details
Provide your account number and branch details to help nodal officers stop outgoing funds.

Three Clear Paths to Reporting

1. Call 1930 Helpline

Best for Financial Fraud. Direct access to the National Cyber Crime portal to freeze scammer accounts.

2. Online Portal

Visit cybercrime.gov.in for all cases including social media hacking, harassment, or data theft.

3. Nearest Cyber Police Station

For serious cases involving large sums or physical threats, visit your local designated Cyber Cell.

What happens after you report?

I4C Processing

The Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C) validates your complaint and alerts the relevant bank or platform.

Funds Freezing

If reported within the "Golden Hour" (first 2 hours), the nodal officer can flag and freeze the stolen amount in the scammer's bank account.

Investigation & FIR

Based on the evidence, your complaint may be converted into an FIR for formal investigation by local police.

Status Tracking

You can track your complaint status using the Acknowledgement Number on the official portal at any time.

Data Preservation
Don't lose your proof!
Never Delete

Keep all chats and call logs, even if you find them distressing.

Screenshots

Capture everything immediately before blocking the scammer.

Email Headers

Forward full email headers for IP and domain tracking.

Bank Records

Save the exact UTR/Transaction ID and timestamp.

Statewise Cyber Helpdesk

Select your state to find dedicated regional contact points and nodal officers.
