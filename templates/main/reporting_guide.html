Detailed Reporting Guide

A comprehensive, step-by-step checklist to help you report cyber incidents correctly and preserve crucial evidence for authorities.

  • Disconnect from Network: Turn off Wi-Fi and unplug ethernet cables immediately to prevent further data exfiltration or malware spread.
  • Lock Accounts: Change passwords for your primary email, banking, and social media from a different, secure device.
  • Freeze Assets: Call your bank's 24/7 fraud helpline to block credit cards, debit cards, and UPI access.
  • Take Screenshots: Capture screenshots of malicious URLs, phishing emails, fake profiles, and unauthorized transactions.
  • Do Not Delete: Do not delete suspicious emails, text messages, or chat histories. They contain metadata essential for investigation.
  • Export Logs: If it's a corporate breach, export server logs, firewall logs, and access records immediately.
  • Call 1930: Dial the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal helpline for immediate assistance with financial fraud.
  • Register Complaint Online: Visit cybercrime.gov.in and file a formal report. Keep the generated reference number safe.
  • Visit Local Cyber Cell: Take printed copies of your evidence and the online complaint reference number to your nearest police cyber cell to file an FIR.
  • Scan Devices: Run a full system scan using reputed antivirus and anti-malware software.
  • Implement 2FA: Enable Two-Factor Authentication on all recovered and critical accounts using an authenticator app.
  • Monitor Credit: Check your CIBIL score regularly for the next 12 months to detect any unauthorized loans taken in your name.
