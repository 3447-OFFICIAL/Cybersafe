Digital Identity

Digital Identity Protection

Your online footprint is your resume to the world. Protecting it requires more than just a password — it demands a defensive architecture.

Standard Protective Protocol
MFA Integration

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (App-based, not SMS) on all primary accounts — banking, email, and social.

Credential Management

Use a premium password manager to generate and store 16+ character unique passphrases for every service.

Software Hardening

Set OS and critical applications to auto-update to patch zero-day vulnerabilities before they are exploited.

Breach Monitoring

Financial Safety

Financial Assets Security

Digital banking and UPI have made transactions instant — but require instant alertness to prevent unauthorized transfers and UPI fraud.

Financial Defense Protocol
OTP Integrity

Never share OTPs with anyone claiming to be a bank official, telecom company, or government authority — OTPs are always final-step secrets.

UPI QR Verification

Always verify the registered name before confirming any UPI payment. Scammers generate spoofed QR codes with similar-looking merchant names.

Transaction Alerts

Enable SMS and push alerts for every transaction above ₹1. Immediately call 1930 if any unauthorized debit occurs.

Vishing Awareness

Investment scams promising "guaranteed returns" through WhatsApp or Telegram are India's #1 financial cyber fraud vector in 2024.

Device Security

Device Integrity Shield

Your device is the primary attack surface. Hardening it against APK-based malware, spyware, and remote-access tools is the first line of defense.

Device Hardening Protocol
Sideloading Prevention

Never install APKs from outside official stores. 73% of Android malware in India spreads through WhatsApp-forwarded APK files.

Screen Recording Block

Disable "Display over other apps" permissions for any app that doesn't require it. RAT malware exploits this vector to steal banking credentials.

Permission Audit

Monthly review of all app permissions — especially SMS, Contacts, Camera, and Microphone access which are commonly abused by stalkerware.

Encryption & Lock

Enable full-device encryption and use biometric + strong PIN (6+ digit) lock. Never use pattern locks on public-facing devices.

Network Safety

Network Defense

Unsecured networks are the easiest point of entry for man-in-the-middle attacks targeting OTPs and session tokens on public Wi-Fi.

Network Security Protocol
VPN Encryption

Use a zero-log VPN when connecting to public Wi-Fi. Never access banking portals or OTP flows on open networks.

Router Hardening

Change default router admin password, disable WPS, and update firmware quarterly. Use WPA3 encryption where available.

DNS Security

Configure encrypted DNS (DNS-over-HTTPS) using Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 or Google 8.8.8.8 to prevent DNS spoofing attacks.

Personal Safety

Personal Data Hygiene

Oversharing on social platforms and messaging apps creates a detailed intelligence profile that scammers exploit for social engineering.

Personal Data Protocol
Social Media Footprint

Audit and restrict privacy settings on all platforms. Never post your Aadhaar, PAN, address, or phone number in public posts or groups.

Vishing Defense

Hang up on unexpected calls claiming to be CBI, police, or TRAI threatening "digital arrest." India's law enforcement never calls to demand payment.

Phishing Recognition

Check sender domain carefully — scammers use domains like "support-sbi-in.com" vs real "sbi.co.in". Never click links in unsolicited SMS or email.

Telegram/WhatsApp Scams

Investment groups on messaging apps offering 30-200% returns are fraud operations. India lost ₹7,061 crore to online investment scams in 2023 alone.

Incident Response

What To Do Immediately After a Cyber Attack

Time is critical. Every minute of delay allows attackers to deepen access or transfer funds. Follow this exact protocol.

NATIONAL CYBERCRIME HELPLINE
1930
Call immediately to freeze fraudulent transactions

Reporting within the golden hour (first 60 minutes) gives the highest probability of recovering stolen funds. The Citizen Financial Cyber Fraud Reporting and Management System allows banks to freeze transactions in the fraud chain in real-time.

01

Call 1930 — Immediately

Report financial fraud to freeze fund transfers before they reach mule accounts. Have your transaction ID, bank account number, and timestamps ready.

02

File on cybercrime.gov.in

Submit a formal complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal. For financial fraud, use the "Financial Fraud" category. Screenshot all evidence before filing.

03

Revoke All Active Sessions

Log out of all devices on Google, WhatsApp, banking apps. Change passwords from a clean, trusted device — not the compromised one. Enable new MFA codes.

04

Preserve Evidence

Screenshot all suspicious messages, transaction IDs, caller numbers, and UPI IDs involved. Do not delete anything — this is admissible evidence in cybercell FIR proceedings.

05

File FIR at Local Cyber Cell

Visit your nearest Cyber Crime Cell or local police station to file a formal FIR. Bring all evidence, the online complaint reference number, and identity documents.
