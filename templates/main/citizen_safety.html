Official Safety Guide

Citizen Safety

Comprehensive guidelines, response directives, and hardening protocols to protect your identity, devices, and financial assets from modern cyber threats.

Digital Identity Protection

Your online footprint is your resume to the world. Protecting it requires more than just a password; it requires a defensive mindset.

Standard Protective Protocol:
MFA Integration

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (App-based) on all primary accounts.

Software Hardening

Set your OS and critical applications to "Auto-Update" to patch vulnerabilities.

Credential Management

Utilize a premium password manager to generate and store 16+ character passphrases.

Financial Assets Security

Digital banking and UPI have made transactions instant, but they also require instant alertness to prevent unauthorized transfers.

Financial Defense Protocol:
OTP Integrity

Never share One-Time Passwords with anyone, including individuals claiming to be bank officials.

UPI Vigilance

Remember: You only enter your UPI PIN to SEND money, never to receive it.

Transaction Auditing

Set up instant SMS/Email alerts and review your bank statements weekly.

Network Perimeter Defense

Your home Wi-Fi is the primary entry point for your smart devices. Secure the perimeter to prevent eavesdropping and data theft.

Network Hardening Protocol:
Encryption Standard

Use WPA3 or WPA2-AES encryption. Change default router admin credentials immediately.

SSID Strategy

Disable SSID broadcasting and use a generic name that doesn't reveal your identity or location.

Secure Transit

Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks. Use a trusted VPN if connection is mandatory.

Personal Data Privacy

Social engineering is the most common entry point for hackers. Protecting your personal information is as much about behavior as it is about technology.

Behavioral Defense Protocol:
Information Siloing

Avoid sharing sensitive details like mothers maiden name or location on public social profiles.

Stranger Vigilance

Never accept friend requests from unknown profiles. Verify identity of callers requesting data.

Hardware Integrity Shield

Your physical devices are the endpoints of your digital life. Hardening them ensures that even if stolen, your data remains encrypted.

Hardware Hardening Protocol:
Disk Encryption

Enable BitLocker or FileVault. Mobile devices are encrypted by default if using a passcode.

Biometric Locks

Use Fingerprint or Face ID for authentication. Ensure your PIN is a random 6+ digit number.

Identity Document Shield

Your Aadhaar and PAN are the keys to your legal and financial existence. Securing them is paramount to preventing loan frauds and impersonation.

Identity Protection Protocol:
Biometric Locking

Use the mAdhaar app to lock your biometrics. Unlock them only when you need to authenticate.

E-KYC Auditing

Periodically check your Aadhaar authentication history for unknown logins.

Immediate Steps if Compromised

Follow these crucial steps to mitigate damage and secure your digital presence.

Critical Safety Checklist:
Turn off Wi-Fi & Mobile Data immediately
Do NOT restart the device (preserves evidence)
Unplug Ethernet cables from the machine
Isolate from shared home networks
Enable Airplane Mode on mobile devices
Disconnect external hard drives/USBs

Account Hardening Steps:
Change Email & Banking passwords first
Logout from all active sessions remotely
Use unique 12+ character passphrases
Check for unknown recovery emails
Setup physical 2FA keys if available
Enable App-based 2FA immediately

Reporting & Evidence:
Keep logs of all suspicious transactions
Email cybercrime.gov.in with artifacts
Screenshot threats and IP address logs
Request bank to freeze sensitive cards
Call National Helpline at 1930
Notify local Cyber Police Station
