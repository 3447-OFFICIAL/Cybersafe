The Data Supply Chain: How Breaches Become Scams

🌐
Initial Breach
A service you use is hacked, exposing your email, password, and phone number.
🛒
Data Brokering
Your info is bundled into "dumps" and sold in bulk on underground forums.
🔍
Credential Stuffing
Bots test your exposed password across hundreds of banking and email sites.
📞
Social Engineering
Scammers use your leaked details to impersonate banks or police via calls.
💰
Financial Loss
Accounts are drained, or identity is stolen to take out loans in your name.
Defense Protocols
🔗
Delink Stale Accounts
Delete accounts on websites you no longer use. Unsubscribe from newsletters and remove saved payment methods from minor apps.
🔑
Rotate Passwords
Never reuse passwords. If an old service is breached, hackers will test that password on your primary banking and email accounts.
🛡️
Stay Alert & Enable 2FA
Always enable Two-Factor Authentication (Authenticator apps > SMS). Be highly suspicious of urgent calls requesting OTPs or money.
