How to Lessen Phone Call Scams

The Golden Rule

"Any call that creates urgency, fear, or asks for an OTP, Aadhaar, or money — is a scam. Legitimate organisations always give you time. Scammers never do. When in doubt, hang up."

Before a Scam Call

Protect yourself in advance

  • Register on TRAI DND: Legally block commercial spam by registering on trai.gov.in.
  • Check SIM connections: Visit sancharsaathi.gov.in to block unknown SIMs linked to your Aadhaar.
  • Avoid public listings: Never post your primary banking number on OLX or Facebook Marketplace.
During the Call

What to do in the moment

  • Hang up immediately: Banks or CBI never use "digital arrest" or demand OTPs over video calls.
  • Never dial USSD codes: Codes starting with **, *401*, or *67* silently hijack your calls.
  • Call back officially: Pressure is proof of a scam. Always look up official helpline numbers yourself.
After the Call

Act fast, time matters

  • Report on Chakshu: Use sancharsaathi.gov.in/Home/Chakshu to help TRAI block the scammer.
  • Call 1930 immediately: If you shared any detail, speed is vital to freeze fraudulent transactions.
  • File National Complaint: Register crime details at cybercrime.gov.in to create legal records.

Risks of Number Compromise

A compromised phone number is the primary target for advanced social engineering and financial fraud.

CRITICAL
Digital Arrest Scam

Caller poses as CBI, ED, or TRAI officer and claims your number is linked to money laundering. They place you under a fake "digital arrest" over video call and extort money. India's #1 fastest growing scam in 2025.

CRITICAL
OTP Harvesting

Fraudster calls pretending to be your bank or UIDAI and tricks you into sharing your OTP verbally. Every UPI fraud, every bank account takeover in India starts with this single step.

CRITICAL
SIM Swap via Aadhaar Leak

Using your leaked Aadhaar details, attackers visit a telecom store and get a new SIM issued on your number. Your original SIM goes dead. They now receive all your bank OTPs.

HIGH RISK
Call Forwarding Activation

You're tricked into dialing a USSD code like **21*<number># which silently forwards all your calls to the scammer. You receive no notification. Your OTPs go directly to them.

HIGH RISK
TRAI Disconnection Threat

Scammer calls claiming your number will be disconnected in 2 hours due to "illegal usage." Panic makes victims share KYC details, Aadhaar numbers, or OTPs immediately.

HIGH RISK
UPI Collect Request Fraud

Victim receives a UPI collect request disguised as a refund or prize. Entering your PIN to "receive" money actually sends it. Extremely common on PhonePe, GPay, and Paytm in India.

Defensive Strategy

Prevention Tips

Set a SIM PIN

Lock your SIM card with a PIN via your phone settings to prevent it from working in a different device.

DND Activation

Register on the 'National Do Not Call' registry to reduce exposure to marketing databases.

Use Auth Apps

Switch from SMS 2FA to Authenticator Apps (Authy, Google Auth) wherever possible.

Emergency Protocol

Mitigation Tips

Report to TAFCOP

Flag unauthorized connections on the Sanchar Saathi portal for immediate carrier action.

Alert Your Carrier

If you lose signal suddenly, call your carrier from another phone to report a potential SIM swap.

Police Complaint

Lodge a cyber-cell complaint if your number is used in any fraudulent financial transactions.
